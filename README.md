# Personal Finance Analysis (July 2025 – December 2025)

## Overview
This project analyzes six months of personal credit card transactions to identify spending patterns, category concentration, behavioral trends, and opportunities to reduce unnecessary expenses.

> **Note:** Cashback rewards are excluded from this analysis.

## Tools & Technologies
- Excel
- postgreSQL

## Dataset
My own credit card statements

---

## Key Metrics

- **Total Spending:** $1,769.38  
- **Average Monthly Spending:** $294.90  
- **Highest Spending Month:** December ($460.11)  
- **Lowest Spending Month:** October ($184.01)  
- **Trend:** Volatile with seasonal spikes (September and December)

---

## Daily Spending Behavior

- **Highest Spending Day:** Wednesday ($360.09)
- **Highest Transaction Count:** Wednesday (12 transactions)
- **Lowest Spending Day:** Friday ($161.76)

### Behavioral Insights
- Weekdays have higher transaction frequency.
- Weekend purchases tend to be larger per transaction.
- Wednesday spending appears routine-based.
- Saturday spending is lower in volume but higher per purchase.

---

## Optimization Opportunities

- Reduce merchandise spending by 15% → Potential savings of ~$124 (over 6 months)
- Set a monthly spending cap for online marketplaces
- Implement a 48-hour delay rule for non-essential purchases
- Pre-plan seasonal spending to reduce December spikes

---

## Conclusion

Spending patterns show:

- High concentration in discretionary categories
- Significant reliance on e-commerce platforms
- Seasonal and event-driven volatility
- Predictable essential cost baseline (utilities, insurance, internet)

Targeted reductions in merchandise and online purchases would significantly improve financial efficiency.
